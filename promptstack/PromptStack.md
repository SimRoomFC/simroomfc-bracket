# PromptStack — 150 AI Prompts to Run Your Marketing

**The solo founder's AI marketing team. Paste, tweak the brackets, ship.**

Version 1.0 · Works with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and any modern AI.

---

## How to use this pack

Every prompt has **[BRACKETS]** — swap those for your own details, then paste
the whole thing into your AI. That's it.

**Two rules that 10x your output:**

1. **Feed it context first.** Before the prompt, paste one line about your
   business: `My business: [WHAT YOU SELL] for [WHO]. Voice: [3 ADJECTIVES].`
   The AI reuses it for everything after.
2. **Ask for options, then edit.** Every prompt here ends by asking for 3–5
   variations. Pick the best line, not the first one.

## 01 · Positioning & Messaging (18 prompts)

**1.1 — One-liner that makes people get it**
> You are a positioning strategist. My business: [WHAT YOU SELL] for
> [WHO IT'S FOR]. The #1 pain I remove is [PAIN]. Write 5 one-line
> descriptions I could put at the top of my homepage. Each must be under 12
> words, avoid jargon, and lead with the outcome — not the mechanism.
> After the list, tell me which one is strongest and why.

**1.2 — Find the words my customers actually use**
> Here are 5 things customers have said about [PRODUCT]: [PASTE QUOTES /
> REVIEWS]. Extract the exact phrases and emotional words they use. Give me a
> "swipe list" of 15 phrases I can reuse in my copy so it sounds like them,
> not like marketing.

**1.3 — The "before vs after" story**
> My product is [PRODUCT]. Describe my customer's world in two columns:
> BEFORE (their frustrating status quo, in visceral detail) and AFTER (life
> once the problem is solved). 5 bullet points each. Make BEFORE sting and
> AFTER feel earned, not hyped.

**1.4 — Differentiation against a specific competitor**
> My competitor is [COMPETITOR] and they win on [THEIR STRENGTH]. I'm better
> at [MY EDGE]. Write 3 positioning angles that concede their strength
> gracefully while making my edge the thing that actually matters to [WHO].

*…plus 14 more: elevator pitch, category design, objection reframes, "we're
the X for Y" formulas, tagline generator, value-prop teardown, and more.*

## 02 · Content & Social (24 prompts)

**2.1 — A week of posts from one idea**
> Turn this one idea into a week of content: [IDEA]. Give me 5 posts (Mon–Fri)
> for [PLATFORM]. Vary the format: one hot take, one how-to, one story, one
> list, one question that sparks replies. Match this voice: [VOICE]. Keep each
> under [WORD COUNT] words and end 3 of them with a soft CTA to [ACTION].

**2.2 — The scroll-stopping hook machine**
> Write 15 opening lines (hooks) for a post about [TOPIC]. Use these hook
> types, at least 3 each: contrarian, curiosity gap, specific number,
> personal confession, "here's what nobody tells you." No clickbait I can't
> back up. Rank them 1–15 by scroll-stopping power.

**2.3 — Turn a blog post into 8 social posts**
> Here's a long piece: [PASTE ARTICLE OR LINK SUMMARY]. Atomize it into 8
> standalone social posts. Each should make one point, work without the
> original, and be formatted for [PLATFORM]. Include 2 that are just a single
> punchy quote.

**2.4 — Reply that grows the account**
> A big account posted: "[THEIR POST]". Write 3 replies that add real value,
> subtly show my expertise in [MY FIELD], and don't come off as promotional.
> Each under 280 characters.

*…plus 20 more: carousel scripts, thread outlines, caption + hashtag sets,
"reply guy" templates, content pillars, repurposing engine, trend-jacking
safely, and a 30-day calendar generator.*

## 03 · Email & Newsletters (20 prompts)

**3.1 — Welcome sequence that converts**
> Write a 4-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to [LIST/PRODUCT].
> Email 1: deliver the promised thing + set expectations. Email 2: the story
> of why I built this. Email 3: my single best free tip. Email 4: soft pitch
> for [OFFER]. Voice: [VOICE]. Give me subject lines with 2 options each.

**3.2 — Subject lines worth testing**
> Write 12 subject lines for an email about [TOPIC]. Mix curiosity, benefit,
> and urgency styles. Keep them under 45 characters so they don't truncate on
> mobile. Flag any that might trip spam filters.

**3.3 — The re-engagement "breakup" email**
> Write a win-back email to subscribers who haven't opened in 60 days. Warm,
> a little vulnerable, gives them one reason to stay and an easy way to leave.
> Subject line should feel personal, not automated.

*…plus 17 more: launch sequences, cart-abandon emails, monthly newsletter
template, product-update announcements, survey emails, and a cold-outreach
sequence that isn't spammy.*

## 04 · Sales & Landing Pages (22 prompts)

**4.1 — Full landing page draft**
> Write a landing page for [PRODUCT] aimed at [WHO]. Sections: hero
> (headline + subhead + CTA), 3 problem bullets, 3 benefit blocks, how it
> works in 3 steps, social-proof placeholder, FAQ (5 Qs), and a final CTA.
> Voice: [VOICE]. Lead with outcomes, keep sentences short.

**4.2 — Objection-crusher FAQ**
> List the 8 real reasons someone would hesitate to buy [PRODUCT] at
> [PRICE]. For each, write a FAQ answer that's honest, removes the fear, and
> nudges toward yes — without sounding defensive.

**4.3 — Pricing page copy**
> I have 3 tiers: [TIER DETAILS]. Write the pricing page copy: a one-line
> value statement per tier, a "best for" label, feature bullets phrased as
> outcomes, and a line that makes the middle tier the obvious choice.

*…plus 19 more: sales page long-form, VSL script, checkout-page reassurance
copy, guarantee statement, urgency without sleaze, upsell/downsell scripts,
and a "why now" section generator.*

**5.2 — Meta/Instagram ad set**

> Match voice: [VOICE].

## 06 · Customer & Ops (16 prompts)

**6.1 — Support reply that keeps the customer**
> A customer wrote: "[MESSAGE]". Write a reply that's warm, takes

**6.2 — Turn a happy customer into a testimonial**
> A customer said they love [PRODUCT] because [REASON]. Write a short message
> asking for a testimonial that makes it easy for them — include 3 quick
> prompt questions they can answer instead of a blank page.

*…plus 14 more: refund responses, onboarding checklists, review-request

## 07 · Strategy & Planning (12 prompts)

**7.1 — 90-day marketing plan on one page**
> I run [BUSINESS] and want to reach [GOAL] in 90 days. Give me a one-page
> plan: 3 focus channels, weekly actions, one metric per channel, and the

**7.2 — Audit my funnel for leaks**
> My funnel: [DESCRIBE STEPS + ANY NUMBERS]. Point out the 3 biggest likely
> leak points, why each leaks, and one specific fix per leak I could ship
> this week.
